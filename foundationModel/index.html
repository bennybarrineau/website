Foundation Models

Building a strong foundation for the future of AI

What are Foundation Models?

By definition Foundation Models are : A large scale neuron network trained on vast amounts of data that serve as a base or foundation for a multitude of applications. 
Foundation models are utilized for language generation by training large-scale models on a diverse range of internet text to learn grammar, facts, reasoning abilities, and biases.

How Foundation Models work.

Transfer Learning

Able to apply information that it has learned from one situation to a different situation that it was not trained in.

Transformers

AI architecture that can turn heaps of raw data (text or audio) into a compressed representation that captures the data’s basic structure.
A prime example is the utilization of NASA’s large amount of earth science data.

Fine Tuning

Fine tuning a foundation model can help build upon the model to perform different tasks entirely which cuts down on time and resources needed.
Fine-tuning involves taking a pre-trained model and adapting it to perform a specific task by training it on a task-specific datasets.

Utilization

Foundation models can be used to cut out a lot of manual effort by extracting the structure of raw data so that fewer labeled examples are needed.

How NASA uses Foundation Models

Satellite Imaging

NASA has a foundation model that uses satellite images to map out areas of
natural disasters and help measure the future predictions of risk areas.

Crop Prediction

Like the example of the NASA model, it can be repurposed, with fine tuning, and
be redeployed for tasks like tracking deforestation or predicting crop yields.
